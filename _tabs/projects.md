### [Climate Vulnerability Assessment, Alexandria, Egypt](https://www.burohappold.com/projects/climate-vulnerability-assessment-framework-for-egypt/)

Egypt's escalating climate risks and fragmented data were constraining local governments' ability to assess and act on vulnerabilities across governorates. Co-developed a Climate Vulnerability Assessment tool based on Bloomberg Associates and the Greater London Authority's *A Spatial Analysis of Climate Risk Across Greater London: Methodology Report (2024 Update)*, integrating geospatial analysis and qualitative inputs across Exposure, Physical Sensitivity, Socio-Economic Sensitivity, and Adaptive Capacity dimensions, delivered alongside capacity-building workshops and piloted in the Governorate of Alexandria. The tool equipped stakeholders to identify vulnerability hotspots, prioritise adaptation interventions, and establish a replicable model for locally-led vulnerability assessment across Egypt.

### [Thermal Comfort Guidelines, Toronto, Canada](https://www.burohappold.com/projects/city-of-toronto-thermal-comfort-guidelines/)

No precedent existed for inclusive, co-designed thermal comfort guidelines at city scale despite growing extreme heat pressure on Toronto's public spaces. Delivered simulation-driven heat-mitigation assessments anchored by a co-design process engaging the City's Technical Advisory Committee, Indigenous communities, the development industry, researchers, academics, and the broader public. This collaborative process produced North America's first public-realm thermal comfort guidelines, giving planners and policymakers a community-grounded framework for embedding heat resilience into urban development.

### [Wind Microclimate Analysis, London Wall West, United Kingdom](https://www.burohappold.com/projects/london-wall-west/)

Complex mixed-use redevelopment in the City of London required rigorous wind microclimate assessment to meet planning requirements and protect public realm quality. Delivered wind microclimate analysis ensuring compliance with City of London Wind Microclimate Guidelines, recommending targeted green and blue infrastructure strategies to mitigate wind risks and enhance public realm resilience. Analysis contributed directly to securing Planning Committee approval, demonstrating the policy and planning value of evidence-based microclimate assessment.

### Liveability Index, Athens, Greece

> In Athens, tourism-driven demand is pushing local resources past their carrying capacity, with the heaviest strain falling on neighbourhoods least equipped to adapt.

> Athens, like many fast-growing tourism cities, is making decisions about housing, water, and neighbourhood liveability with [data that sits in disconnected silos](/assets/pdf/dashboard-liveability.pdf).
{: .prompt-warning }

Tourism-driven demand is pushing Athens' resources beyond carrying capacity thresholds. Conducted GIS-based composite index analysis integrating heat exposure, water demand, tourism intensity, and cooling access to reveal where high exposure and low adaptive capacity converge, producing spatially precise evidence to prioritise adaptation interventions in the city's most vulnerable neighbourhoods.

### Lessons for Policymakers

Short-term rental platforms, water utilities, the national statistics office, and municipal planners each hold pieces of the picture, but no one holds the whole. The result is policy that arrives late, often after the damage to housing affordability and resource access has already taken shape.
This pilot showed what becomes possible when those pieces are brought together responsibly. By combining short-term rental density, water consumption, building permits, air arrivals, and census data at the neighbourhood scale, central Athens can be read as a system rather than a series of disconnected pressures. Neighbourhoods most exposed to tourism saturation, infrastructure strain, and climate risk become visible, and so do the trade-offs between visitor growth and resident wellbeing.
Three actions for city policymakers:

1. Treat data sharing as part of tourism governance, not a side activity. Build standing agreements with short-term rental platforms, utilities, and statistical agencies so that planners do not have to rebuild the evidence base every time a new question arises.
2. Set neighbourhood-level thresholds for tourism saturation, water stress, and housing pressure, and publish them. Visible thresholds give residents, investors, and operators a shared reference point and make policy responses predictable.
3. Resource a small data stewardship function inside the municipality. Its role is to broker access, protect privacy, and translate findings into language mayors and councils can act on.
